Guitar Triad Shapes

Each triad can be voiced 3 ways (inversions) depending on which note sits in the bass (root, third, or fifth), giving 6 moveable shapes per string group. R = root · 3 / b3 = major/minor third · 5 = fifth.

All seven diatonic triads of a key, grouped into three hand positions. Each position is anchored on one inversion of the I chord (highlighted); the other chords use whichever inversion sits closest to that anchor, keeping your hand in roughly one place on the neck. To practice something musical, choose a progression other than "All Chords".
